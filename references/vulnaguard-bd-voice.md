# Vulnaguard BD Email Voice

Use this whenever writing external Vulnaguard emails: solicitation responses, RFI responses, partnership outreach, or follow-ups from leads. This is NOT the SeanBuilds voice — same directness, different objective.

---

## The Persona

Vulnaguard is a small, focused cybersecurity compliance firm. We specialize in CMMC and NIST SP 800-171 work for defense contractors and small businesses trying to get certified without getting buried.

**Mental model:** We're not a big consulting firm sending a proposal deck. We're the people who actually do the work, and we've seen enough of this space to know what trips companies up. We show up like a knowledgeable peer who's been through the weeds — not a vendor pitching features.

**Tone:** Professional but direct. No corporate boilerplate. No promises we can't keep. The reader should feel like we read their situation, not their RFP template.

---

## The Standard Opening Formula

Every external BD email — regardless of type — opens with three things in order:

1. **Who we are** — Vulnaguard, what we do in one sentence
2. **What we noticed / why we're reaching out** — tied to their specific situation, solicitation, or ask
3. **How we can help** — concrete, not generic

Never lead with a company bio paragraph. Lead with their situation first, then position us in relation to it.

---

## Email Types

### Solicitation Response (RFP / IFB)

**Objective:** Win the contract. Answer their specific ask. Show we understand the scope better than a generalist.

**Rules:**
- Reference the solicitation number and agency in the subject line
- Open by naming the specific need they described, not our capabilities
- Keep the intro to 2-3 sentences. Let the attached proposal do the heavy lifting.
- Close with a clear next step (call, submission deadline acknowledgment, or contact point)
- Attach the formal response document. The email body is a cover note only.

**Subject line format:**
`[Company] Response — [Solicitation Number] | [Agency Short Name]`

**Cover note template:**
```
[Agency/Contact],

Vulnaguard is a cybersecurity compliance firm focused on CMMC and NIST 800-171 assessments for defense contractors. We're responding to [solicitation number / title].

[One sentence: what specifically about their situation or scope we're addressing.]

Attached is our formal response. We're available at [contact] if you have questions ahead of the deadline.

Sean Murrill
Vulnaguard
seanmurrill@vulnaguard.com
```

---

### RFI Response

**Objective:** Establish expertise. Help them understand the problem space. Don't pitch — educate and let them pull us in.

**Rules:**
- Treat it like a conversation, not a brochure
- Be specific about what we've seen in the field, not what our service includes
- Don't push for a call unless they invited it
- Close with an offer: "Happy to answer specific questions about your environment"

**Subject line format:**
`Re: [RFI Title / Number] — Vulnaguard Input`

---

### Partnership / Relationship Outreach

**Objective:** Open the door. Establish mutual value. Suggest a low-pressure next step.

**Rules:**
- Peer-level tone. Not a pitch, not a cold sales email.
- Name exactly why you're reaching out to this specific organization (not a mass email)
- Describe the overlap or complementary nature — what we each bring
- Suggest a short call, not a demo or proposal
- Keep it under 150 words in the body

**Subject line format:**
`[First Name] — [short, specific hook]`

Example: `Marcus — CMMC referral partnership`

**Body template:**
```
[Name],

Vulnaguard handles CMMC Level 2 and NIST 800-171 work for small defense contractors. We noticed [specific reason you're reaching out to them].

[One sentence on the overlap or why it makes sense to connect.]

Worth a 20-minute call to see if there's a fit? No agenda — just want to understand what you're working with.

Sean Murrill
Vulnaguard
```

---

### Follow-Up (Post-Solicitation or Post-Intro)

**Objective:** Stay on their radar without being annoying. Reinforce one specific thing.

**Rules:**
- One paragraph max
- Reference the last touchpoint specifically
- Offer something: an answer, a resource, a concrete next step
- Never a generic "just checking in"

---

## Voice Calibration

| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| "We are pleased to submit..." | "Here's our response to [solicitation]" |
| "Our team of experts..." | "We've done this for [type of client]" |
| "Comprehensive solution" | "Handles [specific thing]" |
| "We look forward to the opportunity" | "Happy to answer questions before [deadline]" |
| "Robust compliance framework" | "A clear path to certification" |
| "Best-in-class" | Cut it entirely |

---

## What Doesn't Change From SeanBuilds

- No em dashes
- Short sentences
- Lead with the reader's situation, not ours
- No buzzwords
- Outcome before feature

---

## Self-Check Before Sending

1. Does the opening reference their specific situation, not a generic intro?
2. Did I lead with who we are in one sentence, not a paragraph?
3. Is the call to action clear and specific?
4. Could a 10-year compliance veteran at a prime contractor read this and not cringe?
5. Is the email under 200 words for outreach, under 100 for follow-ups?

# SeanBuilds Voice & Persona Skill

## The Persona

The builder who explains complicated things like you're standing in the garage together.

- Smart enough to build the engine
- Practical enough to explain why it matters
- Honest enough to tell you when you don't need a bigger engine at all

**Mental model:** Sean is never the consultant in the room. He's the guy who already built the thing, noticed what broke, and is now explaining it over coffee.

---

## Voice Principles

**Simple. Direct. Practical. Builder-minded. Outcome-focused.**

Every sentence should pass this test: *Would a builder explaining this to a frustrated business owner say it this way?*

If it sounds like a consulting firm → rewrite it.
If it sounds like a builder explaining something over coffee → publish it.

---

## Word Rules

### Never Use
- Digital transformation
- Synergy
- Cutting-edge
- Disruptive
- Agentic workflow
- Revolutionary
- Leverage / leveraging
- Operationalize
- Robust solution
- Seamlessly

### Prefer Instead
- Save time
- Reduce busywork
- Better visibility
- Simpler process
- Real problem
- Practical solution
- Fix / build / solve
- Make it work
- Fewer clicks
- Less frustration

## Storytelling Framework

All content follows this arc — not always explicitly, but always structurally:

**Situation → Frustration → Observation → Build → Result**

1. **Situation** — Here's a real thing that happens
2. **Frustration** — Here's why it's annoying / expensive / broken
3. **Observation** — Here's what most people miss about why
4. **Build** — Here's what we actually did
5. **Result** — Here's what changed

The SeanBuilds Formula shorthand: **What's broken? Why? Simple analogy. Show the fix. Show the outcome.**

---

## Humor Guidelines

Observational only. Real-world situations that everyone recognizes.

✅ "If it takes 14 clicks, something is broken."
✅ "Most companies don't need more AI. They need fewer headaches."
❌ Comedian setup/punchline format — if it sounds like a joke, rewrite it as an observation

---

## Analogies Library

Draw from these or invent new ones in the same register:

- "We serve burgers, not hot dogs or tacos." (focus / scope)
- "Don't reinvent the wheel. Just make a better wheel." (pragmatism)
- "A dashboard isn't the answer; it's a window." (tools vs. outcomes)
- "Imagine hiring a world-class mechanic and giving them a butter knife instead of a wrench." (internal tools)
- "AI is a hammer. A hammer doesn't build a house. Somebody still needs a plan." (AI reality check)
- "Compliance is a lot like checking your smoke detectors." (boring-but-necessary)
- "I don't start with code. I start with annoyance." (how good software gets built)

## Content Format by Channel

### LinkedIn
- Talk about real problems and lessons learned
- Avoid hype
- No buzzwords
- First-person founder voice
- End with an insight, not a pitch
- Short paragraphs — one idea per line

### Blog Posts
Structure: **Start with something annoying → Explain it → Use an analogy → Show the fix**
- Open with a frustration, not a definition
- Introduce analogies early to anchor the reader
- Concrete before abstract

### Emails
- Short, direct, respectful
- Focus on outcomes
- No long preambles
- Lead with what matters to the reader

### Landing Pages
- Lead with the headache, not the technology
- Answer in order: Why care → How can you help → Can you do it → Why are you different
- Feature mentions come *after* the result is established

### Product Messaging
- Lead with results, then features
- Never lead with what the product *is* — lead with what changes for the user

### Video Scripts / Voiceover
- Write as spoken, not read
- Short sentences
- Rhetorical questions are fine ("Sound familiar?")
- Use pauses structurally — write `[beat]` where breathing space should live

### Case Studies
Structure: **Problem → Constraints → Build → Outcome → Lessons Learned**

## What Sounds Like Sean

> "If it takes 14 clicks, something is broken."

> "Most companies don't need more AI. They need fewer headaches."

> "I don't start with code. I start with annoyance. Show me the spreadsheet everyone hates."

> "You're not selling software. You're selling less frustration, better visibility, more time, fewer clicks, and simpler processes."

## What Doesn't Sound Like Sean

> "Leveraging AI-powered transformational synergies to optimize operational efficiency."

> "Our cutting-edge platform delivers robust, scalable solutions for the modern enterprise."

> "We help businesses digitally transform their operations through disruptive innovation."

---

## Self-Check Before Publishing

Before finalizing any content, run through:

1. Does this lead with the frustration or headache?
2. Is there a concrete analogy anchoring the concept?
3. Does it sound like someone explaining this over coffee, or like a brochure?
4. Are there any banned words? (transformation, synergy, disruptive, etc.)
5. Does it tell you the outcome before the feature?
6. Would Sean read this and say "yeah, that sounds like me"?

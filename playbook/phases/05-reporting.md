# Phase 5: Reporting

**Objective:** Produce, QA-review, and deliver all engagement deliverables.

---

## Entry Criteria

- Phase 4 quality gate passed
- Validated findings list and draft executive summary complete

---

## Activities

### 5.1 Draft Technical Report
- Use the technical report template
- Include: scope, methodology, findings (ordered by severity), evidence references, appendices
- Each finding entry must include: title, severity, affected asset/control, description, evidence, recommendation
- Do not include false positives or informational items unless specifically requested

### 5.2 Draft Executive Summary
- Use the executive summary template
- 1–2 pages maximum
- Audience: CEO, board member, or non-technical decision maker
- Include: engagement purpose, key findings narrative (no technical jargon), overall risk posture, top 3 priorities for remediation
- No CVE IDs, no IP addresses — use plain language

### 5.3 Draft POA&M (Compliance Engagements)
- Use the POA&M template
- One row per open finding
- Include: control ID, finding description, responsible owner, planned completion date, milestones, estimated cost (if known)
- Sort by risk level (Critical first)
- POA&M should be realistic — if a finding will take 12 months, say 12 months

### 5.4 Draft Risk Register (Where Applicable)
- Use the risk register template
- Summarizes the top risks by business impact
- Useful for executive risk assessments and security program assessments

### 5.5 Draft Executive Presentation (Where Applicable)
- 10–15 slides maximum
- Opening: engagement overview and who was assessed
- Body: top findings, risk posture, framework scorecard (compliance engagements)
- Close: prioritized remediation roadmap, next steps
- Vulnaguard branding applied

### 5.6 Internal QA Review
- Lead consultant completes the QA checklist
- A second reviewer checks the final package before client delivery
- QA checklist must be signed off before anything goes to client

### 5.7 Client Draft Review (Optional but Recommended)
- Share draft report with client's primary technical contact
- Allow 3–5 business days for review
- Address factual corrections only — do not change severity ratings based on client pushback without documented justification

### 5.8 Issue Final Report Package
- Apply "FINAL" designation to all file names
- Deliver via secure email or client-approved file sharing
- Confirm receipt in writing

### 5.9 Findings Review Meeting
- Schedule a 60–90 minute meeting to walk through findings with client stakeholders
- Cover: top findings, risk ratings, remediation priorities, POA&M plan
- Record action items

---

## Outputs

- Final technical report (Word + PDF)
- Final executive summary (Word + PDF)
- Final POA&M (Excel or Word, per client preference)
- Final executive presentation (PowerPoint, if applicable)
- Risk register (if applicable)

---

## Deliverables to Client

- Full report package
- Findings review meeting

## Common Issues

**Client pushes back on a severity rating:** Listen to their context. If they provide new information that genuinely changes the risk, adjust the rating and document the rationale. If they are just uncomfortable with the finding, hold the rating and explain the methodology.

**Report is too long:** Cut informational findings from the main body — move to an appendix. Executive summary should never exceed 2 pages.

**Client wants a verbal-only delivery (no written report):** Do not agree to this. A written report is a contractual deliverable and protects both parties.

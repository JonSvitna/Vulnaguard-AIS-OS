## 7. Personnel & Key Personnel

Subcontractor will provide qualified personnel to perform the SOW. **Key
Personnel** named in the SOW may not be substituted without the Prime's
prior written consent, except for death, disability, or termination of
employment. Personnel processing CUI under this Subcontract are
U.S. persons (22 CFR § 120.62).

---

## 8. Security & Data Handling

### 8.1 CUI / FCI

Subcontractor will safeguard any Federal Contract Information (FCI) per
**FAR 52.204-21** and any Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) per
**NIST SP 800-171** and any clauses listed in §6 above.

### 8.2 Incident Reporting

Subcontractor will report any actual or reasonably suspected cyber
incident affecting Prime or Government information **within seventy-two
(72) hours** in accordance with **DFARS 252.204-7012(c)** if applicable.

### 8.3 Sentinel Platform

Where the SOW uses the Vulnaguard Sentinel platform to store or process
CUI, Subcontractor represents that the platform's CUI environment is
configured consistent with NIST SP 800-171 and that all CUI is segregated
from non-CUI tenants.

## 10. Intellectual Property

### 10.1 Government Rights

The Government's rights in deliverables are governed by the Prime
Contract's data-rights clauses (e.g., FAR 52.227-14 or DFARS 252.227-7013
/ 7014). Subcontractor will mark restricted-rights and limited-rights
data appropriately.

### 10.2 Vulnaguard Tools

Vulnaguard retains all rights in its pre-existing tools, methodologies,
templates, and the **Sentinel** platform. Vulnaguard grants the Prime
(and through it, the Government to the extent required by the Prime
Contract) a non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use Vulnaguard tools
embedded in deliverables solely as necessary to use those deliverables
for their intended purpose.

## 12. Limitation of Liability

**Subcontractor's aggregate liability under this Subcontract will not
exceed the greater of (a) the fees paid or payable to Subcontractor in
the twelve (12) months preceding the claim, or (b) $10,000.** Neither
Party is liable for indirect or consequential damages. The cap does not
apply to (i) Subcontractor's indemnification obligations, (ii) breach of
confidentiality, or (iii) gross negligence or willful misconduct.

---

## 13. Indemnification

Subcontractor will indemnify Prime against third-party claims arising
from Subcontractor's gross negligence or willful misconduct. Prime will
indemnify Subcontractor against claims arising from Prime's modifications
to Subcontractor deliverables, Prime's data, or Prime's use of
deliverables in violation of this Subcontract.

---

## 14. Termination

### 14.1 For Convenience

Prime may terminate this Subcontract for convenience on **thirty (30)
days'** written notice, provided Prime pays Subcontractor for all services
performed and deliverables accepted through the termination date, plus
non-cancellable third-party commitments.

### 14.2 For Default

Either Party may terminate for material breach uncured within thirty (30)
days of written notice.

### 14.3 Termination of Prime Contract

If the Prime Contract is terminated, Prime will give Subcontractor prompt
notice. Subcontractor is entitled to payment for work performed and
accepted through the effective date of termination.

---

## 15. Dispute Resolution

The Parties will first attempt to resolve disputes through executive
discussion within thirty (30) days of written notice. Unresolved disputes
relating to interpretation of the Prime Contract or Government claims
flow through the Prime in accordance with the **Disputes** clause of the
Prime Contract. All other disputes will be resolved in the courts
identified in §17.

---

## 16. Independent Contractor

Subcontractor performs as an independent contractor. Nothing in this
Subcontract creates a partnership, joint venture, agency, or
employer-employee relationship.

# Security Policy Development Runbook

**Service Code:** POLICY
**Typical Duration:** 3–6 weeks (depends on number of policies)
**Phases:** Discovery → Planning → Assessment → Analysis (policy drafting) → Reporting (client review) → Closeout

---

## Service Description

Vulnaguard develops custom security policies tailored to the client's environment, regulatory requirements, and business context. Policies are written in plain language, aligned to the applicable framework, and designed to be actually used — not filed and forgotten.

This service is most commonly triggered when a gap assessment identifies missing or inadequate policy documentation.

---

## Policy Priority Tiers

Not all clients need all policies. Scope based on framework requirements and client maturity.

### Tier 1 — Foundation (Required for CMMC L2, NIST 800-171)
- Information Security Policy
- Access Control Policy
- Incident Response Policy and Plan
- Configuration Management Policy
- System and Communications Protection Policy
- Media Protection Policy
- Physical Protection Policy

### Tier 2 — Program Maturity
- Risk Management Policy
- Vulnerability Management Policy
- Patch Management Policy
- Change Management Policy
- Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery Policy
- Third-Party / Vendor Risk Policy

### Tier 3 — Advanced / Regulatory
- Data Classification and Handling Policy
- CUI Handling Policy (CMMC-specific)
- Acceptable Use Policy
- Remote Work Policy
- Mobile Device Policy
- Encryption Policy
- Audit and Logging Policy

## Discovery Additions

- [ ] All existing policies and procedures (even informal ones)
- [ ] Regulatory requirements driving the policy need (CMMC, HIPAA, PCI, state law)
- [ ] Organizational structure and policy ownership (who approves policies?)
- [ ] Any prior assessments identifying specific policy gaps
- [ ] Employee handbook or HR policies (to check for conflicts)

---

## Assessment Phase — Policy Gap Analysis

Before drafting, assess existing policies:

For each required policy:
- [ ] Does a policy exist?
- [ ] Is it current (reviewed within 12 months)?
- [ ] Does it cover the required control elements for the applicable framework?
- [ ] Is it actually implemented and followed?

Document gaps — these become the drafting priorities.

---

## Drafting Standards

Every policy Vulnaguard produces must include:

- **Purpose:** Why this policy exists
- **Scope:** Who and what it applies to
- **Policy Statements:** The actual requirements (clear, action-oriented)
- **Roles and Responsibilities:** Who owns what
- **Enforcement:** Consequences for non-compliance
- **Review Cycle:** How often the policy is reviewed and by whom
- **Version History:** Date, version number, author, approver

**Length target:** 2–5 pages per policy. Not a 40-page document no one reads.

---

## Review Process

### Internal Draft Review
- Lead consultant reviews draft against framework requirements
- Confirm all required control elements are covered

### Client Review
- Send draft to client's designated reviewer (typically IT Director and/or Legal)
- Allow 5 business days for review
- Conduct a 30-minute review call to walk through feedback
- Incorporate factual corrections; push back on changes that remove required control elements

### Final Approval
- Client's authorized signer approves the policy
- Record approver name and date on the policy document
- Issue final version

## Post-Delivery Guidance

Policies are only useful if they're implemented. At closeout, provide the client with:

- A policy implementation checklist (what needs to happen for each policy to go live)
- Guidance on communicating new policies to staff
- Recommended review cadence (annually for most policies)
- Recommendation to store policies in Sentinel for ongoing tracking

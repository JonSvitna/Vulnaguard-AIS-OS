# Executive Risk Assessment Runbook

**Service Code:** ERA
**Typical Duration:** 2–3 weeks
**Phases:** Discovery → Planning → Assessment → Analysis → Reporting → Closeout

---

## Service Description

An Executive Risk Assessment translates technical security findings and threat context into business risk language for a board or executive team. The focus is on decision-making — helping leadership understand what risks they own, what they would cost, and what they should do about them.

This service is ideal as a standalone engagement for organizations with limited security maturity, or as an executive-facing layer on top of a completed technical or compliance assessment.

---

## When to Use This Service

- New CISO or security leader needs to brief the board
- Executive team wants to understand their cyber risk posture before a transaction (M&A, fundraise)
- Organization has received a cyber insurance renewal questionnaire or premium increase
- Board requested a cybersecurity briefing after a high-profile industry incident
- Post-assessment when the technical findings need to be translated for leadership

---

## Discovery Additions

- [ ] Business objectives and top revenue-generating systems
- [ ] Any known prior incidents (internal or vendor-caused)
- [ ] Cyber insurance coverage and limits
- [ ] Regulatory environment (HIPAA, DFARS, PCI, state breach notification laws)
- [ ] Key vendors with system access
- [ ] Board composition and existing cybersecurity awareness level

---

## Assessment Activities

### Business Objectives Review
- Identify the 3–5 most critical business processes and the systems that support them
- Understand what "a bad day" looks like: ransomware, data breach, operational outage

### Threat Analysis
- Research the client's industry threat landscape (relevant threat actors, TTPs)
- Review CISA advisories, recent sector-specific incidents
- Map top threats to the client's environment based on discovery

### Risk Workshop
- Facilitated 90-minute session with executive team
- Walk through the top 5–7 risks identified
- Quantify impact in business terms: estimated downtime cost, regulatory fine, reputational damage, recovery cost
- Prioritize by likelihood and business impact
- Capture risk appetite and tolerance from leadership

### Technical Validation (if applicable)
- If no prior technical assessment exists, conduct a lightweight external reconnaissance to ground the risk narrative
- This is not a full VA — it's enough to make the risk discussion concrete

---

## Risk Quantification Approach

Frame risks in terms executives understand:

- **Financial impact:** Estimated cost of a ransomware event, data breach notification, regulatory fine
- **Operational impact:** Estimated downtime in hours/days, business processes affected
- **Reputational impact:** Likelihood of public disclosure, customer notification requirements
- **Insurance gap:** What the current policy covers vs. the estimated loss scenario

Use publicly available breach cost benchmarks (IBM Cost of a Data Breach Report, Verizon DBIR) to anchor estimates. Always disclose these are estimates, not guarantees.

## Board Presentation Structure

1. Cybersecurity Landscape Overview (2 slides — the threat environment their industry faces)
2. Our Environment Today (3 slides — current posture, top risks, what we found)
3. Business Impact Analysis (2 slides — what a bad day looks like, quantified)
4. Priority Actions (2 slides — top 3 things leadership should resource immediately)
5. Recommended Next Steps (1 slide)

Keep it under 15 slides. Executives do not read appendices.

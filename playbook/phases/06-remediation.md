# Phase 6: Remediation (Advisory)

**Objective:** Provide expert guidance to the client as they address findings. Scope is defined in the SOW — not all engagements include this phase.

---

## Entry Criteria

- Final report delivered and acknowledged
- Remediation advisory hours included in SOW

---

## Scope Note

Vulnaguard's role in this phase is advisory. We guide, review, and answer questions. We do not implement fixes on the client's behalf unless that is explicitly scoped as a separate managed services engagement.

---

## Activities

### 6.1 Remediation Kickoff
- After the findings review meeting, schedule a separate remediation planning call
- Walk through the POA&M and help the client assign owners and realistic timelines
- Identify any Quick Wins the client can close in the first 30 days

### 6.2 Ongoing Advisory Check-Ins
- Cadence defined in SOW (typically bi-weekly)
- Agenda: POA&M status update, blockers, technical questions
- Document each call in the engagement folder

### 6.3 Evidence Review
- As clients close POA&M items, they submit evidence of remediation
- Review evidence against the original finding description
- Acceptable evidence types vary by finding — document what was accepted and why

### 6.4 POA&M Updates
- Update item status after evidence review: Open → Closed, or Open → In Progress
- Do not mark an item Closed without evidence on file
- Share updated POA&M with client after each review cycle

---

## Outputs

- Updated POA&M (version-controlled)
- Advisory call notes
- Evidence of remediation (filed in engagement folder)

---

## Deliverables to Client

- Updated POA&M after each review cycle

---

## Quality Gate

- [ ] All Critical and High findings have a documented remediation plan with owner and date
- [ ] No item closed without evidence on file
- [ ] Client has confirmed they understand the POA&M process

---

## Hours Tracking

Track advisory hours against the SOW allocation. Notify client when 75% of hours are consumed. Issue a change order to add hours before the allocation is exhausted.

---

## Common Issues

**Client closes findings without evidence:** Do not update POA&M status. Explain what evidence is required and request it.

**Client deprioritizes Critical findings:** Document your advisory in writing (email). Your role is to advise, not enforce — but ensure your recommendation is on record.

**Client wants you to implement the fix:** If this is out of scope, offer a change order. Do not absorb out-of-scope work for relationship reasons.

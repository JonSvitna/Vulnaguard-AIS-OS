**Hook:** Bad email addresses were retrying forever — one regex now routes permanent failures to 'cancelled' instead of back into the send queue.
**Talking points:**
1. Before: every failed send reset to 'drafted', meaning bad addresses hit the queue again and again burning send credits and masking the real problem
2. The fix: a single regex distinguishes permanent failures (invalid address, domain not found, 422) from transient ones (rate limit, network blip) — only transient failures revert to drafted for retry
3. Lesson: failure states need at least two buckets — 'try again' and 'stop trying' — and conflating them turns a one-time error into an infinite loop

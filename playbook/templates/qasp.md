## 2. Purpose

This QASP establishes the methods the COR (or designated buyer
representative) will use to monitor Vulnaguard's performance under the
referenced SOW, verify that deliverables meet the agreed performance
standards, and document acceptance for invoicing under the Subcontract or
Purchase Order.

This QASP is **not** a contract document; it does not change the SOW or
introduce new requirements. Where a buyer's own QASP is supplied, that
QASP controls; this template is offered as a starting point.

---

## 4. Surveillance Methods

The buyer will use a combination of the following methods:

- **100% inspection** — every deliverable reviewed against acceptance
  criteria in the SOW and standards in §3.
- **Random sampling** — for repeating activities, a sample of work product
  selected at the buyer's discretion.
- **Periodic status review** — `[weekly | biweekly]` status meetings with
  the COR; written status note within 24 h after the meeting.
- **Customer complaints / feedback** — escalations from end users or
  stakeholders impacted by the work.
- **Self-reporting** — Vulnaguard's monthly performance report (§5).

---

## 5. Performance Reporting

Vulnaguard will submit a written **Monthly Performance Report** to the COR
within five (5) business days after the end of each month, covering:

1. Status of each deliverable in §3 (planned vs. actual);
2. Open POA&M items, owners, target dates, and trend;
3. Any deviations from the schedule or scope and their root causes;
4. Risks identified and mitigation actions;
5. Personnel changes, if any;
6. Recommended changes (each requires a Change Order per SOW §10).

---

## 6. Records & Documentation

The COR will maintain a surveillance file containing:

- Signed Deliverable Acceptance Forms (per `docs/deliverable_acceptance.html`);
- Monthly Performance Reports;
- Any written complaints, defects, or remarks and the disposition of each;
- Change Orders;
- Invoices and acceptance status.

Vulnaguard will retain its underlying engagement records for **24 months**
after final acceptance, available for the COR's reasonable inspection
during normal business hours on five (5) business days' notice.

---

## 7. Remedies for Non-Conformance

For non-conformance against the standards in §3:

1. **Notify** the contractor in writing within five (5) business days of
   discovery, citing the specific standard, ID, and observed shortfall.
2. **Cure period** — Vulnaguard has the cure period stated in §3 (typically
   5–10 business days) to bring performance into conformance at no
   additional cost.
3. **Schedule slip** — schedule slip caused by Vulnaguard may trigger a
   recovery plan; slip caused by buyer (late evidence, unavailable
   subjects) extends the schedule day-for-day per SOW §5.
4. **Fee adjustment** — if non-conformance is uncured after the cure
   period, the buyer may withhold the deliverable's milestone payment
   until cure, or, for repeated material non-conformance, pursue
   termination for default under the Subcontract.
